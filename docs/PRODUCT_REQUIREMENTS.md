# 1. Product Vision

Drum Family OS exists to become the operational backbone for a modern network of music schools.

Drum Family is not building another generic CRM. The goal is to create an operating system that helps a music school network grow, manage quality, coordinate people, retain students, and make better decisions from one connected system.

The first customer is Drum Family itself. The product must solve real operational problems inside the existing Drum Family network before it becomes a platform for other music schools.

In the long term, Drum Family OS should support any music school that needs a scalable system for lead management, scheduling, learning operations, payments, analytics, community, and growth.

# 2. Business Goal

The core business problem is that a growing school network cannot be managed effectively through scattered chats, spreadsheets, calendars, payment tools, manual reports, and disconnected services.

Drum Family OS should help the business:

- Increase the number of active students.
- Reduce lead loss between first contact and first lesson.
- Improve student retention and reactivation.
- Standardize school operations across branches.
- Give owners and managers accurate operational visibility.
- Reduce manual administrative work.
- Improve teacher coordination and accountability.
- Make franchise expansion repeatable.

The system should turn fragmented operational activity into a connected growth engine for the school network.

# 3. North Star Metric

The North Star Metric is:

**Active Students**

An active student is a student who is currently engaged with the school through paid lessons, an active subscription, scheduled learning activity, or another approved active status defined by the business.

This metric matters because it reflects the health of the entire operating system: acquisition, conversion, scheduling, learning experience, retention, payments, community, and branch performance.

Metrics that influence active students:

- New leads created.
- Lead response time.
- Lead-to-trial conversion rate.
- Trial-to-paid conversion rate.
- Number of scheduled lessons.
- Lesson attendance rate.
- Student retention rate.
- Student churn rate.
- Reactivated students.
- Teacher availability and utilization.
- Payment completion rate.
- Subscription renewal rate.
- Branch capacity utilization.
- Student satisfaction.
- Referral rate.
- Community engagement.

# 4. Пользователи

## Owner

The owner needs strategic visibility across the network: active students, revenue, branch performance, teacher performance, growth bottlenecks, retention, and expansion readiness.

Primary needs:

- Understand whether the network is growing.
- See which branches are healthy or at risk.
- Identify operational bottlenecks.
- Make investment and hiring decisions.
- Track progress toward business goals.

## Branch Manager

The branch manager is responsible for the performance of a specific school or location.

Primary needs:

- Manage branch capacity.
- Track leads, trials, students, schedules, teachers, and payments.
- Control service quality.
- Coordinate administrators and teachers.
- Report branch performance to the owner.

## Administrator

The administrator manages the daily operational flow between leads, students, parents, teachers, schedules, and payments.

Primary needs:

- Process incoming leads quickly.
- Book trials and regular lessons.
- Maintain student records.
- Communicate with students and parents.
- Track payments and attendance.
- Prevent missed lessons, lost leads, and operational gaps.

## Teacher

The teacher delivers the learning experience and influences retention, progress, and student loyalty.

Primary needs:

- See schedule and student information.
- Track attendance.
- Understand student goals and progress.
- Prepare for lessons.
- Communicate relevant updates.
- Reduce administrative overhead.

## Student

The student is the core value recipient of the platform.

Primary needs:

- Attend lessons consistently.
- Understand their learning path.
- Track progress.
- Stay motivated.
- Participate in community activities.
- Continue learning with the school over time.

## Parent

The parent often influences payment, retention, schedule decisions, and satisfaction for younger students.

Primary needs:

- Understand schedule and payment status.
- See progress and attendance.
- Receive important updates.
- Trust the quality and consistency of the school.

## Franchisee

The franchisee operates under the Drum Family model and needs repeatable systems, reporting, and operational guidance.

Primary needs:

- Launch and operate a branch using proven processes.
- Follow network standards.
- Access operational analytics.
- Manage staff, students, payments, and growth.
- Reduce dependency on informal knowledge transfer.

# 5. Главные бизнес-процессы

## От заявки до постоянного ученика

This process covers the full path from first interest to a stable active student.

Core stages:

- Lead capture.
- Lead qualification.
- First response.
- Trial lesson booking.
- Trial lesson attendance.
- Trial feedback.
- Offer and payment.
- Regular schedule assignment.
- First month retention.
- Active student status confirmation.

Key product question:

How can the system minimize lead loss and increase conversion into active students?

## От ученика до амбассадора

This process covers student growth, emotional attachment, community participation, referrals, and long-term loyalty.

Core stages:

- Consistent attendance.
- Visible progress.
- Personal learning goals.
- Participation in events.
- Community engagement.
- Performance opportunities.
- Referral behavior.
- Ambassador status.

Key product question:

How can the system help students stay motivated long enough to become loyal advocates of the school?

## Работа преподавателя

This process covers the teacher's operational and educational workflow.

Core stages:

- View schedule.
- Prepare for lessons.
- Mark attendance.
- Track progress.
- Add notes or recommendations.
- Report issues.
- Support retention through quality teaching.

Key product question:

How can the system reduce administrative load for teachers while improving student progress and retention?

## Работа администратора

This process covers daily school operations.

Core stages:

- Process leads.
- Manage communication.
- Book and reschedule lessons.
- Track payments.
- Maintain student and parent data.
- Coordinate with teachers.
- Monitor operational exceptions.

Key product question:

How can the system help administrators prevent missed actions, lost leads, unpaid lessons, and schedule conflicts?

## Работа собственника

This process covers strategic and operational control of the network.

Core stages:

- Monitor active students.
- Monitor revenue and payments.
- Compare branch performance.
- Identify growth bottlenecks.
- Review teacher and administrator performance.
- Make hiring, marketing, and expansion decisions.

Key product question:

How can the system give the owner a reliable operating picture without manual reporting?

# 6. MVP

## Что обязательно входит

The MVP must focus on the shortest path to increasing and managing active students.

Required MVP scope:

- Unified student profile.
- Lead capture and lead status tracking.
- Trial lesson booking.
- Conversion tracking from lead to trial to paid student.
- Basic schedule for lessons.
- Teacher assignment.
- Attendance tracking.
- Active student status.
- Basic payment status tracking.
- Administrator workflow dashboard.
- Branch manager operational dashboard.
- Owner dashboard focused on active students.
- Basic analytics for conversion, attendance, retention, and active students.
- Manual data correction by authorized users.
- Role-based access at a basic operational level.

## Что НЕ входит

The MVP must not attempt to become the full future platform.

Out of MVP scope:

- Full public marketplace.
- Full community platform.
- Advanced AI automation.
- Native mobile applications.
- Franchise management portal.
- Complex billing engine.
- Payroll management.
- Advanced LMS functionality.
- Student gamification.
- Full marketing automation.
- Multi-country localization.
- Deep external integrations.
- Automated legal document workflows.
- Advanced financial accounting.
- Custom website builder.

# 8. Product Principles

## Каждый экран помогает принять решение

Every screen must help a user understand what is happening, what matters, and what action should be taken next.

The system should avoid passive data storage. It should turn information into decisions.

## Одна система вместо множества сервисов

Drum Family OS should reduce fragmentation across chats, spreadsheets, calendars, payment trackers, and manual reports.

The product should become the operational source of truth for the school network.

## Automation First

Routine workflows should be designed with automation in mind from the beginning.

Automation should reduce missed actions, manual reporting, repeated communication, and operational delays.

## AI First

AI should be treated as a product capability, not a decorative feature.

AI should help with prioritization, risk detection, recommendations, summaries, and operational support after the core data model is reliable.

## Scalable by Design

The system should support growth from one network customer to multiple branches, franchisees, and eventually other music schools.

Scalability means operational repeatability, clear roles, clean data, modular architecture, and measurable workflows.

# 10. Risks

## Product Risks

- The product becomes a generic CRM instead of a music school operating system.
- MVP scope becomes too large.
- The team optimizes for features instead of active students.
- User roles are described abstractly but not validated in real operations.
- The system fails to become the source of truth.
- Teachers and administrators reject the product due to added workload.
- AI features are introduced before the data model is trustworthy.

## Business Risks

- Drum Family operational processes are not standardized enough to productize.
- Branches work differently and require too much customization.
- Franchise needs diverge from the first customer needs.
- The product does not produce measurable improvement in active students.
- Manual habits remain stronger than platform adoption.

## Technical And Data Risks

- Incorrect definition of active student creates misleading analytics.
- Poor data quality damages trust in dashboards.
- Schedule, payment, and attendance data become inconsistent.
- Access control is underestimated.
- Integrations with existing tools become more complex than expected.
- Future support for other schools is blocked by assumptions specific to Drum Family.

## Adoption Risks

- Administrators see the system as surveillance rather than support.
- Teachers do not maintain progress or attendance data.
- Owners continue relying on manual reports.
- Parents and students do not receive enough value from the system.

## Mitigation Principles

- Validate workflows before implementation.
- Define active student precisely before building analytics.
- Keep MVP focused on lead-to-active-student conversion and retention.
- Measure adoption by role.
- Introduce automation only where the workflow is already understood.
- Treat AI as an assistant to reliable operations, not a replacement for them.
